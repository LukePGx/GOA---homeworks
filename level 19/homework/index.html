About Bananas

Bananas are elongated, edible fruits produced by several kinds of large herbaceous flowering plants in the genus Musa. In some countries, bananas used for cooking may be called plantains.

Bananas are high in potassium and have good levels of dietary fiber, vitamin C, and vitamin B6. They are also rich in carbohydrates and low in fat.

Types of Bananas

  • Cavendish Banana: The most common type, known for its yellow skin.
  • Red Banana: Smaller and sweeter than the Cavendish, with red skin.
  • Plantain: Larger and starchier, often used for cooking.
